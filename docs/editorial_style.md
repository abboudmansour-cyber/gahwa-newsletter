# Gahwa Newsletter — Editorial Style Guide

> Last updated: 2026-05-07

## Core Identity

Gahwa is the GCC's Morning Brew — a daily, AI-generated business newsletter that delivers sharp, data-driven market intelligence to Saudi and regional executives. Named after the traditional Arabic coffee (Gahwa), the newsletter aims to be a daily ritual for decision-makers.

---

## Tone

- **Authoritative but accessible** — Write for MDs and CEOs, but avoid unnecessary jargon.
- **Confident and direct** — No hedging language ("might", "could possibly"). State facts with conviction.
- **Slightly irreverent, never disrespectful** — Morning Brew-style wit is welcome, but respect Saudi cultural norms.
- **Data-driven** — Every claim should be supported by a number, statistic, or cited source where possible.
- **Urgent but not alarmist** — Convey market tempo without fear-mongering.

---

## Pacing

- **Short paragraphs** — 2–3 sentences max per paragraph.
- **White space is your friend** — Readers scan; make it easy to skim.
- **Bold key numbers** — E.g., "$8.7B inventory gap", "37% YoY growth".
- **Bullet points for lists** — Never dense paragraph blocks.
- **One idea per section** — Each section should have a singular focus.

---

## Writing Style

- **Active voice** — "PIF launched" not "A launch was made by PIF".
- **Present tense preferred** — "Saudi Arabia invests" not "Saudi Arabia has been investing".
- **Strong opening sentences** — Hook the reader in the first 8 words.
- **Transition phrasing** — Use bold intermediate headers like **The Big Picture**, **By the Numbers**, **Why It Matters**.
- **Avoid AI-isms** — No "In today's rapidly evolving landscape", "Let's dive in", "It's worth noting".
- **Direct address** — Use "you" sparingly; prefer "GCC markets", "regional leaders", "executives".

## GCC Audience Expectations

- **High context required** — Readers are deeply familiar with the region; don't over-explain KSA basics.
- **Respect hierarchy** — Refer to "HRH", "HE" for royalty and ministers where appropriate.
- **Know the key players** — PIF, Aramco, SABIC, NEOM, Red Sea Global, stc, Almarai, Savola, Majid Al Futtaim.
- **Localize examples** — Use riyals (SAR) not dollars unless comparing globally. Use Saudi time zones.
- **Cultural awareness** — Ramadan, Hajj, National Day, and Saudi weekends (Fri-Sat) affect business cycles.
- **Vision 2030 context** — Frame developments within the Vision 2030 narrative when relevant.
- **Avoid politics** — Geopolitical analysis is fine; partisan commentary is not.

---

## Headline Rules

- **15 words max** — Headlines must be scannable.
- **Active verbs** — "Saudi Arabia Pushes" not "A Push by Saudi Arabia".
- **Include a number when possible** — "3 Charts That Explain the GCC M&A Boom".
- **No clickbait** — Must deliver on the promise.
- **Subheadlines encouraged** — A 10–20 word secondary line for context.
- **Avoid questions in headlines** — Statements are stronger than questions.

---

## Humor Boundaries

- **Light, dry wit** — Comparable to The Economist's humor, not The Onion.
- **Saudi-appropriate** — No alcohol references, no religious satire, no political mockery.
- **Puns are acceptable** — But only if genuinely clever.
- **Avoid sarcasm** — It doesn't land well in cross-cultural B2B contexts.
- **The "Gahwa Break" section** — Reserve humor for this clearly marked space.

---

## Formatting Standards

- **H2 (##)** for main sections.
- **H3 (###)** for subsections within a section.
- **Bold** for key numbers, company names, and data points.
- **Italics** for publication names, foreign terms, and emphasis (rare).
- **Lists** use `-` bullet points.
- **Blockquotes (>)** for pull quotes and key stats.
- **Horizontal rules (---)** to separate major sections.
- **No emojis** in newsletter body (except in the "Gahwa Break" section if thematically fitting).

---

## Language and Terminology

- **Default: English** — Bilingual editions are not currently supported.
- **Use correct local terms**:
  - *Gahwa* — The newsletter name; use contextually
  - *GCC* — Always refer to Gulf Cooperation Council markets
  - *SAR* — Saudi riyal (not SR)
  - *KSA* — Acceptable shorthand for Saudi Arabia
  - *UAE* — Acceptable shorthand for United Arab Emirates
- **Spell out acronyms on first use** — Except GCC, KSA, UAE, PIF, AI.
- **Numbers** — Use commas: 1,000,000. Use "SAR 1.2B" format for billions.
- **Dates** — Format as "May 7, 2026" (not 07/05/2026 or May 7th).

---

## Editorial Constraints

- **No fluff** — If it doesn't inform or engage, cut it.
- **Length target** — 800–1,200 words per edition.
- **Sections per edition** — 5–7 sections (including sponsor slot).
- **Links** — Always cite sources. Hyperlink cleanly (no naked URLs).
- **CTAs** — Every edition must have at least one CTA (subscribe, sponsor inquiry, feedback).
- **No plagiarism** — Rewrite sourced material; never copy-paste.
- **AI disclosure** — Readers should be aware content is AI-generated but not reminded in every edition.
