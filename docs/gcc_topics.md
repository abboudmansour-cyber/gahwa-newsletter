# Gahwa Newsletter — GCC Topic Coverage

> Last updated: 2026-05-07

## Purpose

This document defines the canonical editorial categories for Gahwa Newsletter. Every edition draws from these topics, weighted by current relevance, seasonality, and editorial judgment. For each category we define: why it matters to GCC executives, the target tone for coverage, and example story angles.

---

## 🛢️ Oil

**Why it matters:** Oil is the foundation of the GCC economy. Saudi Arabia's OPEC+ decisions, Brent crude movements, and energy transition strategy affect everything from government spending to mega-project funding. Every GCC executive needs to understand where oil is heading.

**Target tone:** Data-driven, market-focused, never political. Use precise numbers (Brent at $78/bbl, not "oil prices are high"). Avoid speculation on geopolitical motives.

**Example story angles:**
- Saudi production cuts and their impact on global supply balances
- Aramco earnings and dividend sustainability
- OPEC+ meeting previews and post-meeting analysis
- Hydrogen strategy as the post-oil hedge
- ACWA Power renewable megaproject milestones
- Petrochemical margins and downstream diversification

---

## 🏦 Sovereign Wealth

**Why it matters:** GCC sovereign wealth funds — PIF, ADIA, Mubadala, QIA, KIA — manage over $3T in assets. Their investment moves shape global markets, fund domestic giga-projects, and drive economic diversification. This is the money story.

**Target tone:** Institutional, analytical, deal-focused. Track AUM growth, sector allocation shifts, and co-investment patterns. Avoid breathless "PIF invests in everything" coverage.

**Example story angles:**
- PIF domestic investment portfolio (which giga-projects are funded)
- ADIA and Mubadala international co-investments
- QIA's global real estate and energy transition plays
- How oil price cycles affect SWF capital deployment
- SWF hiring and talent competition with global funds
- Return benchmarks and performance comparisons

---

## 🚀 GCC Startups

**Why it matters:** The GCC startup ecosystem is maturing fast. Saudi and UAE lead the region with increasing venture funding, unicorn exits, and government support. This category tracks the region's entrepreneurial engine.

**Target tone:** Energetic but rigorous. Celebrate wins but analyze fundamentals. Avoid hype — not every funding round is "record-breaking."

**Example story angles:**
- Saudi unicorn pipeline (who's next after Tamara, Tabby, Jahez)
- UAE early-stage ecosystem and talent density
- Riyadh vs. Dubai as startup hubs — the competition heats up
- Government-backed VC funds (SVC, PIF's Jada, Mubadala Ventures)
- Exit landscape: IPOs vs. M&A in the Gulf
- Regional expansion stories (GCC startups going global)

---

## 🤖 AI

**Why it matters:** The GCC is making a bet-the-house move on AI. Saudi created SDAIA with royal mandate, the UAE has a Minister of AI, and both are pouring billions into compute, talent, and LLMs. This is the defining technology story in the region.

**Target tone:** Forward-looking, technically literate but not technical. Focus on adoption, investment, and regulatory impact. Don't overhype — track real deployment.

**Example story angles:**
- ALLaaM and JAIS: regional LLM development progress
- PIF and Mubadala AI investment strategies (Sam Altman's $7T chip push)
- Enterprise AI adoption in GCC banking, healthcare, and energy
- SDAIA regulatory framework and data sovereignty rules
- NEOM's cognitive city AI infrastructure
- AI talent: where the GCC is recruiting and how

---

## 🏖️ Tourism

**Why it matters:** Tourism is a core pillar of both Saudi and UAE post-oil strategies. Saudi targets 150M annual visits by 2030. Dubai already leads in global tourism metrics. The sector drives employment, real estate, and infrastructure spend.

**Target tone:** Metric-driven and seasonal. Track visitor numbers, RevPAR, hotel pipeline, and visa policy changes. Avoid promotional travel-writing style.

**Example story angles:**
- Saudi tourist arrival numbers vs. 2030 targets
- Dubai hotel occupancy and RevPAR trends
- Red Sea Global and AMAALA opening timelines
- Hajj and Umrah economic impact
- Riyadh Air launch and airline capacity expansion
- Saudi vs. UAE: competing for the same tourist dollar

---

## 💳 Fintech

**Why it matters:** Fintech is the GCC's fastest-growing regulated sector. Saudi's SAMA sandbox and UAE's VARA are creating regional fintech hubs. BNPL, digital banking, and payments are reshaping consumer finance.

**Target tone:** Regulatory-aware, growth-focused. Track licensing, funding, and adoption metrics. Understand the regulatory sandbox landscape.

**Example story angles:**
- Digital banking profitability: stc bank, Liv, ADIB, Alinma
- Tamara and Tabby: BNPL regulation and path to profitability
- SAMA open banking framework impact
- UAE VARA crypto regulation and exchange licensing
- Mada payments infrastructure modernization
- Cross-border remittance innovation and incumbent disruption

---

## 🚚 Logistics

**Why it matters:** The GCC is building itself into a global logistics hub connecting Asia, Europe, and Africa. Saudi's King Salman Airport logistics zone and UAE's DP World are key moves. Supply chain nearshoring is accelerating post-COVID.

**Target tone:** Infrastructure-focused, trade-data-driven. Track port volumes, corridor developments, and e-commerce logistics.

**Example story angles:**
- DP World global portfolio strategy and Jebel Ali expansion
- Saudi logistics zone development and foreign investment
- India-Middle East-Europe economic corridor progress
- GCC railway network: connecting the peninsula
- E-commerce logistics: the battle between Noon, Amazon, and local players
- Food security logistics and cold chain investments

---

## 🍽️ HORECA

**Why it matters:** The Hotel, Restaurant, and Cafe sector is massive in the GCC — driven by tourism, a young population, and high disposable income. Saudi's entertainment push is adding millions of new F&B consumers. This is the lifestyle economy.

**Target tone:** Consumer-oriented, trend-aware. Track spending data, new concepts, and event-driven demand.

**Example story angles:**
- Riyadh Season and event-driven F&B demand spikes
- GCC restaurant group expansion and IPO pipeline
- Coffee culture: the specialty coffee boom across the Gulf
- Hotel F&B as a revenue driver vs. rooms
- Food delivery market: Jahez, HungerStation, Talabat dynamics
- Labor market impact on HORECA operations (minimum wage, Saudization)

---

## 🌐 Geopolitics

**Why it matters:** GCC geopolitics shapes oil markets, investment flows, and regional stability. Saudi-US-China triangulation, Iran relations, Red Sea security, and the Abraham Accords all affect business conditions.

**Target tone:** Objective, analytical, data-driven. Never partisan. Provide context without editorializing. Focus on business impact, not ideology.

**Example story angles:**
- Saudi-China trade and investment ties deepening
- Red Sea security and shipping insurance costs
- BRICS membership and GCC economic bloc dynamics
- Saudi-Iran rapprochement: trade and investment follow-up
- US-GCC strategic partnership under different administrations
- Abraham Accords: normalized trade and tourism growth

---

## 💰 Public Investment

**Why it matters:** Government spending is the single largest economic driver in the GCC. Saudi's PIF-backed giga-projects, UAE's infrastructure investment, and Qatar's LNG expansion all flow from public capital. This is the macro story.

**Target tone:** Fiscal-focused, project-oriented. Track budget execution, giga-project milestones, and multiplier effects on the private sector.

**Example story angles:**
- Giga-project spending tracker: PIF portfolio execution vs. timeline
- Saudi budget surplus/deficit and non-oil revenue growth
- UAE federal and emirate-level capital expenditure
- PPP frameworks: private sector participation in infrastructure
- Vision 2030 project financing structures
- ROSHN, Diriyah Gate, NEOM: construction progress and contractor awards

---

## 🔄 Regional Trade

**Why it matters:** Intra-GCC trade and global trade corridors are central to regional economic integration. The GCC Customs Union, trade agreements, and new economic corridors reshape how goods move across and through the region.

**Target tone:** Trade-data-focused, integration-aware. Track volumes, tariff changes, and corridor developments.

**Example story angles:**
- Intra-GCC trade growth and non-oil export trends
- GCC Customs Union implementation progress
- India-Middle East-Europe corridor: what it means for Gulf ports
- Saudi regional HQ program: attracting multinational bases
- Free zone evolution: DIFC, ADGM, and Saudi's new zones
- Food import dependencies and regional food security trade

## Seasonal & Calendar-Based Priorities

Certain categories have higher priority during specific periods:
- **Ramadan** — HORECA (consumer spending patterns), Retail
- **Hajj season** — Tourism, Logistics, HORECA
- **OPEC+ meetings** — Oil (production decisions)
- **Year-end** — Sovereign Wealth (annual reviews), Public Investment (budgets)
- **Q1/Q3** — Regional Trade (trade data releases), Public Investment (budget announcements)
- **November-December** — National Days, GCC Summit, year-end economic outlook

---

## Topic Selection Rules

1. **At least one** Oil, Sovereign Wealth, or Public Investment story per edition.
2. **At least one** GCC Startups, AI, or Fintech story per edition.
3. **Balance sectors** — mix traditional (oil, SWF) with forward-looking (AI, startups).
4. **No more than 2 stories from the same category** in a single edition.
5. **Geographic diversity** — cover at least 2 different GCC countries per edition.
6. **Seasonal override** — if a major event or data release occurs, it takes priority over default weights.
